## 为什么植物组织培养必须在超净工作台内进行无菌操作？

第一次参观植物组织培养实验室的人，几乎都会注意到一个共同的现象。

无论是外植体接种、继代培养，还是培养基分装，工作人员通常都会在一个带有透明挡板的工作台内完成操作，而不是直接在普通实验台上进行。

这个设备就是植物组织培养实验室最常见的设备之一——**超净工作台（Laminar Flow Hood）**。

很多刚接触植物组织培养的人都会产生疑问：为什么所有操作都要在这里完成？普通实验台难道不能进行接种吗？

答案与植物组织培养最大的挑战之一有关——**污染控制**。

## 植物组织培养为什么如此害怕污染？

植物组织培养所使用的培养基富含蔗糖、矿物质、维生素以及植物生长调节物质，这些成分不仅能够满足植物组织的生长需求，同时也适合许多细菌、真菌等微生物生长。

与此同时，植物外植体在接种前通常需要经过切割、分离等处理，切口暴露后抵抗外界微生物的能力会明显下降。

如果空气中的细菌或真菌孢子进入培养瓶，就可能在培养过程中迅速繁殖，导致培养材料受到污染，甚至整瓶培养失败。

因此，对于植物组织培养来说，如何降低污染风险始终是整个培养流程中最重要的工作之一。

## 空气中的微生物远比想象中更多

很多人认为，只要实验室看起来干净，就不会发生污染。

实际上，即使肉眼看不到，空气中仍然漂浮着大量细菌、真菌孢子以及各种微小颗粒。

这些微生物平时不会引起注意，但当培养瓶打开、外植体暴露在空气中的几秒钟内，它们就有机会进入培养体系。

对于普通植物来说，这些微生物未必会造成明显影响；但对于植物组织培养中的外植体而言，微量污染都可能影响整个培养过程。

因此，植物组织培养并不是追求“绝对无菌”，而是尽可能降低污染发生的概率，提高培养成功率。

## 超净工作台如何帮助降低污染风险？

超净工作台并不是普通的实验台。

它通过空气过滤系统，将经过高效过滤后的洁净空气持续送入操作区域，形成相对洁净的工作环境。

这种持续、稳定的洁净气流能够减少空气中的颗粒和微生物进入操作区域的机会，从而帮助工作人员完成接种、继代培养、培养基分装等无菌操作。

需要说明的是，超净工作台的作用是帮助降低空气带来的污染风险，而不是保证任何操作都不会发生污染。

真正稳定的植物组织培养，还需要规范的操作流程共同配合。

## 为什么还需要酒精消毒和规范的无菌操作？

很多人会发现，在正式开始接种之前，工作人员还会反复进行酒精消毒。

例如：

- 使用75%酒精擦拭操作台面；
- 对镊子、剪刀等工具进行消毒；
- 穿戴实验服、口罩和一次性手套；
- 按照规范流程完成每一次接种和转接。

这是因为超净工作台只能提供相对洁净的空气环境，而人体、工具以及操作习惯，同样可能成为污染来源。

如果操作人员频繁触碰非无菌区域，或者工具消毒不到位，即使使用超净工作台，也仍然可能发生污染。

因此，现代植物组织培养强调的是**设备与规范操作共同配合**，而不是单纯依赖某一种设备。

## 商业化组培生产为什么更加重视无菌体系？

对于高校实验室来说，一次可能只培养几十瓶材料。

但对于商业化组培基地而言，一次生产往往涉及几千甚至上万瓶培养材料。

即使污染率只有很低的比例，也可能造成较大的生产损失，增加人工成本，并影响整个批次的生产计划。

因此，越来越多商业化组培企业都会建立标准化的无菌操作流程，包括：

- 培养基高压灭菌；
- 培养容器灭菌；
- 超净工作台接种；
- 器具规范消毒；
- 培养环境管理；
- 人员操作规范。

这些环节共同组成现代植物组织培养的无菌体系，也是提高生产稳定性的重要基础。

## 培养容器同样是无菌体系的重要组成部分

除了培养环境和操作流程，培养容器本身也是植物组织培养体系中不可忽视的一环。

目前，植物组织培养常见的PC组培瓶、PC组培试管以及PP培养容器，通常都会先经过高压灭菌，再配合超净工作台完成接种操作。

只有培养容器、培养环境以及规范的无菌操作共同配合，才能建立更加稳定、高效的植物组织培养流程。

随着商业化微繁殖的发展，培养容器已经不仅仅是盛装培养基的工具，而是整个培养体系的重要组成部分。

## 结语

植物组织培养的成功，并不仅仅取决于培养基配方。

从培养容器的灭菌、超净工作台的使用，到规范的无菌操作，再到稳定的培养环境，每一个环节都会影响最终的培养结果。

对于现代植物组织培养和商业化微繁殖生产来说，建立规范、稳定的无菌操作体系，不仅能够帮助降低污染风险，也能够提高种苗质量、生产效率和批次一致性。

## Why Is Aseptic Operation Under a Laminar Flow Hood Essential for Plant Tissue Culture?

Anyone visiting a plant tissue culture laboratory for the first time will quickly notice that almost every critical operation is performed inside a laminar flow hood rather than on an ordinary laboratory bench.

Whether technicians are inoculating explants, transferring plantlets during subculture, or dispensing culture media, the work is almost always carried out under carefully controlled conditions.

This practice exists for one primary reason: **contamination control**.

## Plant Tissue Culture Is Extremely Sensitive to Contamination

Plant tissue culture media contain carbohydrates, mineral nutrients, vitamins, and plant growth regulators that support healthy plant development.

However, these nutrients can also support the growth of bacteria, fungi, and other microorganisms if contamination occurs.

At the same time, explants are usually cut or divided before culture. Their freshly exposed tissues are much more vulnerable than intact plants growing under natural conditions.

Once microorganisms enter the culture vessel, they may multiply rapidly and interfere with normal plant development, sometimes affecting the entire culture.

For this reason, contamination prevention is one of the most important aspects of successful plant tissue culture.

## Air Contains More Microorganisms Than We Can See

Even a clean laboratory contains microscopic particles suspended in the air.

These include bacteria, fungal spores, and other airborne microorganisms that are invisible to the naked eye.

Whenever a culture vessel is opened, even briefly, these particles have an opportunity to enter the culture environment.

Although such microorganisms may have little effect on plants growing outdoors, they can significantly increase contamination risk in sterile tissue culture systems.

Therefore, tissue culture laboratories focus on reducing contamination opportunities rather than attempting to create an absolutely sterile environment throughout the room.

## How Does a Laminar Flow Hood Help?

A laminar flow hood continuously delivers HEPA-filtered clean air across the working area.

This steady airflow helps reduce the number of airborne particles reaching the culture vessels during inoculation and transfer procedures.

It creates a cleaner working environment that supports aseptic techniques and improves the consistency of laboratory operations.

However, the laminar flow hood is only one part of the entire aseptic system.

Successful plant tissue culture still depends on proper laboratory practices and well-trained personnel.

## Proper Aseptic Technique Is Equally Important

Before beginning tissue culture work, laboratory staff typically disinfect the work surface, sterilize forceps and scalpels, wear appropriate protective clothing, and follow standardized operating procedures.

These practices help reduce contamination introduced by tools, hands, or other contact surfaces.

In other words, contamination control depends on both suitable equipment and correct aseptic techniques working together.

## Commercial Micropropagation Requires Standardized Sterile Procedures

Commercial tissue culture facilities often manage thousands or even tens of thousands of culture vessels at the same time.

Even a relatively low contamination rate may result in significant production losses, increased labor costs, and reduced production efficiency.

For this reason, commercial laboratories usually establish standardized aseptic systems that include:

- Autoclave sterilization of media and culture vessels
- Laminar flow hood operations
- Sterilized laboratory instruments
- Standard operating procedures
- Controlled culture environments
- Staff training

Together, these measures improve production consistency and help reduce contamination risk.

## Culture Vessels Are Also Part of the Sterile System

Culture vessels play an essential role in maintaining sterile conditions.

PC tissue culture bottles, tissue culture tubes, and PP culture containers are commonly sterilized before use and then handled under aseptic conditions during inoculation.

Combined with proper laboratory practices and controlled environmental conditions, suitable culture vessels contribute to a more reliable and efficient tissue culture system.

## Conclusion

Successful plant tissue culture depends on much more than culture media alone.

Sterilized culture vessels, laminar flow hoods, aseptic laboratory techniques, and controlled environmental conditions all work together to support healthy plant growth and consistent commercial micropropagation.

Rather than relying on a single piece of equipment, modern tissue culture laboratories achieve reliable results through a complete and standardized aseptic production system.
