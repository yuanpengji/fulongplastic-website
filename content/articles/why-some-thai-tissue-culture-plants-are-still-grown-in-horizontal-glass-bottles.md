## 为什么会出现水平放置的玻璃培养瓶？

在接触泰国出口瓶苗或参观部分泰国组培企业时，很多人都会发现一个非常具有代表性的现象：

部分组培苗仍然采用玻璃培养瓶进行培养，而且培养瓶通常以水平方式摆放。

对于习惯使用PC组培瓶、PP培养容器或组培袋的从业者来说，这种培养方式看起来似乎有些特殊。

实际上，这不仅是部分泰国企业延续至今的生产方式，也反映了植物组织培养行业的发展历程。

## 这种培养方式并非泰国独有

事实上，水平放置的玻璃瓶培养体系并不是泰国特有的生产方式。

在国内植物组织培养行业发展的早期阶段，许多花卉、观赏植物以及经济作物组培项目同样广泛采用类似培养体系。

在当时的技术条件下，玻璃培养瓶具有：

* 透明度高
* 耐高温灭菌
* 材料容易获得
* 使用经验成熟

因此长期成为组织培养生产中的主要培养容器之一。

## 为什么早期大量采用玻璃培养瓶？

在组织培养产业发展的早期阶段，大多数生产工作主要依赖人工完成。

包括：

* 培养基配制
* 接种操作
* 日常管理
* 取苗移栽

当时行业对于自动化、物流效率和规模化生产的要求还没有今天这么高。

玻璃培养瓶能够满足当时的培养需求，因此被广泛应用于各种组培项目。

部分企业采用水平放置培养方式，也与当时的培养架设计、生产流程以及长期形成的管理习惯有关。

## 传统玻璃瓶体系面临哪些挑战？

随着商业化种苗生产规模不断扩大，传统玻璃瓶体系在实际生产过程中逐渐暴露出一些局限性。

## 接种操作要求较高

许多传统玻璃培养瓶瓶体较长、瓶口较窄。

在接种过程中，操作人员通常需要使用较长的枪镊等工具完成操作。

当培养数量达到数千甚至数万瓶时，接种效率和人工成本都会明显增加。

## 取苗过程较为繁琐

对于很多组培企业而言，取苗往往比接种更加耗时。

当瓶内苗体长大后，由于瓶口空间有限，操作人员需要借助长镊子进行取苗。

在部分生产场景中，为了提高效率，也会采用破瓶取苗等方式完成后续操作。

虽然这种方式能够解决问题，但同时也增加了人工成本和后续处理工作量。

## 重量较大

玻璃培养瓶本身重量较高。

当培养数量达到数千甚至数万瓶时，运输、搬运和仓储成本都会明显增加。

## 存在破损风险

玻璃材质虽然具有优异的透明度，但在运输过程中仍然存在一定破损风险。

特别是在长距离运输和出口过程中，需要投入更多包装保护措施。

## 为什么越来越多企业开始采用塑料培养容器？

近年来，塑料组培容器技术不断成熟。

从PC组培瓶、广口培养杯、PP培养容器到热封培养体系和组培袋，产品种类越来越丰富。

企业能够根据不同培养项目选择合适的容器规格。

同时，大部分产品还支持尺寸和结构定制，以满足不同培养需求。

与传统玻璃瓶相比，现代塑料培养容器通常具有：

* 透明度接近玻璃
* 重量更轻
* 不易破损
* 运输成本更低
* 操作更加方便
* 规格选择更加丰富
* 更适合规模化生产

因此越来越多组培企业开始逐步从传统玻璃瓶体系向现代塑料培养容器体系转变。

## 玻璃培养瓶会被完全淘汰吗？

答案并非如此。

玻璃培养瓶并不是落后的培养技术。

在部分特殊项目和历史形成的生产体系中，玻璃培养瓶至今仍然具有实际应用价值。

而现代PC组培瓶、PP培养容器、热封培养体系以及组培袋的发展，则为组培企业提供了更多规格、更高效率以及更加灵活的生产选择。

## 结语

从传统玻璃培养瓶到现代PC组培瓶、PP培养容器、热封培养体系以及组培袋的发展过程，反映了植物组织培养行业不断追求高效率、标准化和规模化生产的发展趋势。

随着塑料组培容器技术不断成熟，越来越丰富的产品规格、更轻的重量以及更加便利的操作体验，正在推动现代组培生产向更高效率的方向发展。

## Why Are Horizontal Glass Bottle Systems Still Used?

When visiting Thai tissue culture facilities or purchasing Thai tissue culture plantlets, many people notice a distinctive practice:

Some plants are still cultured in glass bottles that are placed horizontally rather than upright.

For growers who are accustomed to PC tissue culture bottles, PP culture containers, or tissue culture bags, this approach may seem unusual.

In reality, this production method reflects an earlier stage in the development of the tissue culture industry and remains part of the production workflow in some Thai facilities today.

## This Practice Is Not Unique to Thailand

Horizontal glass bottle culture systems are not exclusive to Thailand.

In the early years of China's tissue culture industry, similar systems were widely used for flowers, ornamental plants, and various agricultural crops.

At that time, glass culture bottles offered several important advantages:

* Excellent transparency
* Resistance to high-temperature sterilization
* Easy material availability
* Well-established production experience

As a result, they became one of the most widely used culture vessels in commercial tissue culture production.

## Why Were Glass Culture Bottles So Widely Used?

During the early development of commercial tissue culture, most production activities relied heavily on manual labor.

These included:

* Media preparation
* Inoculation
* Daily culture management
* Plantlet removal and transplanting

At that stage, the industry placed less emphasis on automation, logistics efficiency, and large-scale production.

Glass bottles met the requirements of the time and became a standard solution for many propagation projects.

The continued use of horizontal bottle systems in some facilities is also related to historical production workflows, equipment layouts, and long-established management practices.

## What Challenges Do Traditional Glass Bottle Systems Face?

As commercial propagation operations expanded, several limitations of traditional glass bottle systems became more apparent.

## Higher Labor Requirements During Inoculation

Many traditional glass bottles have a long body and a relatively narrow opening.

Operators often need long forceps or specialized tools to complete inoculation procedures.

When production volumes reach thousands or tens of thousands of vessels, labor requirements and operational costs increase significantly.

## More Complex Plantlet Removal

Plantlet removal can often be more time-consuming than inoculation itself.

As plants grow larger inside the bottle, operators must use long tools to remove them through the narrow opening.

In some production environments, bottle-breaking methods are occasionally used to improve efficiency, although this also increases handling requirements and waste management costs.

## Greater Weight

Glass bottles are considerably heavier than modern plastic culture containers.

As production volume increases, transportation, storage, and handling costs become more significant.

## Risk of Breakage

Although glass offers excellent visibility, it is vulnerable to breakage during transportation and daily operations.

Additional packaging and protection measures are often required, particularly for export shipments.

## Why Are More Facilities Adopting Plastic Culture Containers?

In recent years, tissue culture container technology has advanced significantly.

Modern options now include:

* PC tissue culture bottles
* Wide-mouth culture cups
* PP culture containers
* Heat-sealing culture systems
* Tissue culture bags

Compared with traditional glass bottles, modern plastic culture containers typically offer:

* Near-glass transparency
* Lower weight
* Improved durability
* Reduced transportation costs
* Easier handling
* More size options
* Better suitability for large-scale production

As a result, many facilities have gradually transitioned from traditional glass bottle systems to standardized plastic container systems.

## Will Glass Culture Bottles Disappear Completely?

Not necessarily.

Glass culture bottles should not be viewed as outdated technology.

In certain specialized projects and long-established production systems, they continue to provide practical value.

At the same time, modern PC tissue culture bottles, PP culture containers, heat-sealing systems, and tissue culture bags provide growers with more flexibility, greater efficiency, and a wider range of production options.

## Conclusion

The transition from traditional glass culture bottles to modern plastic culture containers reflects the industry's ongoing pursuit of higher efficiency, standardization, and large-scale production.

As tissue culture container technology continues to evolve, lighter materials, broader product choices, and improved handling efficiency are helping commercial propagation facilities build more productive and scalable operations.
